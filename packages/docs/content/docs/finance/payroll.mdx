# Payroll

Payroll is still stuck in batch thinking: semi-monthly ACH, wire delays, invoice float, and spreadsheets that pretend people earn in cliffs. Work is continuous. **Pay should be continuous.**

StreamLine Pro turns the stream primitive into an operations surface for companies:

- Fund a **treasury**
- Spin up **streams per worker** (or contractor)
- Let money drip as milestones clear — or as time elapses under policy
- Optionally route yield, keep amounts confidential, or borrow against live cash flow

## What “programmable payroll” means

| Legacy payroll | StreamLine |
| --- | --- |
| Pay period batch | Per-second accrual, gasless settlement |
| Trust the platform escrow | Move shared object is the escrow |
| Hex addresses in HRIS exports | `name@streamline` handles |
| Opaque fees and T+N settlement | Sponsored txs; finality on Sui |

Milestones become **pay periods, delivery gates, or probation cliffs**. The same raise → approve → drip loop HR already understands — except the money moves while the work happens.

## Pro workspace

The Pro app is built for operators:

1. **Onboard** the company (name / brand) and connect via zkLogin or wallet.
2. **Treasury** — hold idle USDC, invest / divest against the yield vault, withdraw when needed.
3. **Streams** — create worker streams from treasury or wallet balance; track state from the indexer.
4. **Overview** — live picture of outgoing cash flow without opening a bank portal.

Workers use the **User** app: raise completions, watch earnings tick, borrow or vault as their policy allows.

## Handles at company scale

Paying `design@acme` energy into `alice@streamline` removes the hex-error tax from payroll ops. Agents and finance scripts use the same resolve path via [`@streamline/sdk`](/docs/sdk).

## Privacy for real employers

Public chain payroll is a non-starter for most companies. Pair Pro streams with [confidential amounts](/docs/privacy/amounts) when compensation must stay between employer and worker — explorers see that settlement occurs; they do not get a public rate card.

## Where this is going

The same rail extends naturally to:

- Contractor networks and agency benches
- Grants and vesting with streaming unlocks
- Subscriptions and retainers as inverted payroll
- Cross-border teams that never touch correspondent banking for the last mile

StreamLine does not replace your GL tomorrow. It replaces the **rail** that makes “we’ll pay you in two weeks” feel inevitable.
